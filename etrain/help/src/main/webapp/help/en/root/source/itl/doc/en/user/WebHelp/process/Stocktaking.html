Stocktaking

Overview

Stocktaking determines the stock levels of material, products, merchandise, resources and other assets for an entire organizational unit of a company. This includes checking and assessment of stock levels on a defined effective date. Typical methods are Perpetual Stocktaking or End-of-Period Stocktaking.

The central subsystem, STT supports many stocktaking processes. For a detailed introduction, see Terms and Definitions. In principle, the following Stocktaking processes are supported:

  • End-of-Period Stocktaking (supported by tracking the "Last Stocktaking Date" of the configured stocktaking object which thus can be identified as already finished (counted) within a mentioned period)
  • Perpetual Stocktaking ad hoc explicit during first storing (determination of the stock level executes during the first storing of Inventory into a considered Location)
  • Perpetual Stocktaking ad hoc explicit during picking (determination of the stock level executes during a Pick operation, respectively during Retrieval operation, provided that the current stock level at the considered Location is zero or nearly zero after the Pick)
  • Perpetual Stocktaking implicit during receiving (counting of Inventory can be caused by other reasons, e.g. while Receiving or clearing of faults. If Inventory is implicitly counted and confirmed, this is booked as implicit Stocktaking)
  • Perpetual Stocktaking with an explicit planned Counting Order (determination of the stock level executes incrementally for the considered group of Locations during the complete accounting year by generating periodic Counting instructions for a subset of the considered Locations or SKUs. Each Stocktaking object is counted, at most, one time inside the considered period).

This subsystem controls stocktaking fulfillment and execution. Stocktaking is initiated by Stocktaking Orders that are managed by the Order Management Subsystem.

For configuration purposes, see the related topic in the Warehouse Administration Guide.

Main responsibilities of Stocktaking:

  • Fulfillment of Stocktaking Orders and creation of Counting Orders
  • Online execution of Counting Orders
  • at a Goods-to-Person Workstation and
  • with mobile support in Person-to-Goods Zones
  • Offline execution of Counting Orders with use of Counting Lists
  • Configuration for different supported stocktaking types
  • Booking and confirmation of Counting results.

Chapter

Description

Stocktaking - Terms and Definitions

Provides business entity definitions and Stocktaking terminology.

Stocktaking Zone Configurations

A Stocktaking Zone represents all Locations grouped based on (comparably) Stocktaking processes and describes all criteria needed for planning and executing a Stocktaking Request for the group of Locations. Each Location can only be assigned to one Stocktaking Zone.

Counting Confirmation Handling

This screen is for configuring bookings and every type of handling based on a counting confirmation. For each required combination of Stocktaking Zone and error codes, an appropriate confirmation handling is assigned.

Counting Orders

A Counting Order is a collection of counting, weighing, metering or estimating instructions at the fulfillment level based on a Stocktaking Order. One Counting Order instruction always refers to a concrete Inventory Unit, respectively Location, inside the considered Stocktaking Zone and defines the details for quantity determination of the considered Stocktaking object.

Counting Operation

Used to perform Counting operations for a specific Counting Order. It provides all functionality for counting purposes at Goods-to-Person Workstations for any Load Unit Types. Features include detailed instructions and information for the counting operation, additional information regarding ordered Source Load Units, and confirmation of the Counting operation.

RF-Counting

Perform Counting operations for Counting Orders in manual areas with Person-To-Goods configuration on RF-Terminal.

Execution of Stocktaking

Describes the process of Perpetual Stocktaking with an explicit planned Counting Order. This consists of Stocktaking Order creation, release of this Order, automatic creation of related Counting Orders, execution of the Counting process, and a final booking of Counting results.
