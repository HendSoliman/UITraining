Description of Warehouse Structure

Purpose

Locations are a central resource for a Warehouse Management System. They represent Storage Locations, as well as Transport Locations.

     
Warehouse Structure

The following structure is used to group locations within a warehouse:

      
Facility

The top level of the hierarchy is the Facility (warehouse, plant). A Facility could be e.g. a warehouse or a distribution center. Transports of Load Units within a Facility are managed and executed by Dematic iQ®. Transports between Facilities are managed by a host system.

      
Area

The Facility is divided into a number of Areas based on their functions. Criteria for Areas are store types such as an automatic high bay store, Manual Areas, Picking sector or Incoming Goods Area.

     
Aisle - Group

Areas may be divided into Groups. A Group could be an Aisles. An Aisle can be an Aisle of a high-bay store or a grid of Shipping lanes in a Shipping Area. Aisles may be locked if, for example, a storage and retrieval machine (SRM) operating in an Aisle has a long-term defect.
In a high-bay store, an Aisle represents the grouping of those Locations arranged at both sides of the Aisle of an SRM.

      
Level - Subgroup

Aisles may be divided into Subgroups. A Subgroup could be a Level. One Level represents the grouping of Locations arranged at both sides of an Aisle at exact one Level.
Levels may be locked if, for example, a Multi-Shuttle operating at a Level has a long-term defect.

      
Location

A Location is the basic spatial unit that can be addressed in a warehouse. A Load Unit is located always at exact one Location which can be transport points or even Virtual Locations. The basic management of a location could be done either with the classic Layout or a dynamic slotting approach.

A Layout describes how a specific Load Unit is booked onto a Location in a fix and defined number of different possible positionings.
The dynamic slotting is used within a Multishuttle Flex area and allow for a more dynamic and flexible storage of cases of various sizes within a multi-deep bay.

      
Zone

Zones group Locations for a specific purpose; Picking, Replenishment, Stocktaking, etc. If required, Zones can group Locations of several Area or Facilities.
