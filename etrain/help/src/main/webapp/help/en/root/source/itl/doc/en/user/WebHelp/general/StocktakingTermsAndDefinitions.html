Stocktaking - Terms and Definitions

Purpose

This section defines basic Stocktaking terminology.

Stocktaking determines the stock levels of material, products, merchandise, resources and other assets for an entire organizational unit of a company. This includes checking and assessment of these stock levels on a defined, effective date. Typical methods are: Perpetual Stocktaking or End-of-Period Stocktaking. The motivation for Stocktaking is mostly an external legal regulation, not the optimization of internal stock control. Although, Stocktaking execution processes (stock counting) improve the accuracy of stock levels and help to support cost savings.

      
Business Objects

Business Object

Description

Stocktaking Zone

Represents all Locations grouped by (comparably) Stocktaking processes and describes all criteria needed for planning and executing a Stocktaking Request for the group of Locations. Each Location can only be assigned to one Stocktaking Zone.

Stocktaking Order

A Stocktaking-specific specialization of a general Order with a general lifecycle, as described in Order Management. A new Stocktaking Order represents the request to perform Stocktaking operations in a considered Stocktaking Zone for specific Stocktaking objects. It is represented at the Order Fulfillment level by related Counting Orders which define the actual Counting execution tasks.

Counting Order

A collection of counting, weighing, metering or estimating instructions at the fulfillment level based on a Stocktaking Order. One Counting Order instruction always refers to a concrete Inventory Unit, respectively Location, inside the considered Stocktaking Zone and defines the details for quantity determination of the considered Stocktaking object.
     
Terms

Term

Definition

Stocktaking Object

Abstraction for the objects of Counting and Booking confirmation operation. Provides information of it's last Stocktaking operation and can be a:

  • Location (typically a Storage Location of type "bin").
  • Load Unit (contains Inventory Units and is stored on a Location).
  • Inventory Unit (smallest unit of Inventory managed by the system. Specifies the SKU, quantity, Load Unit, additional Inventory attributes, and last stocktaking date).

End-of-Period Stocktaking

Method to determine stock levels (quantities) in context of a basic Stocktaking process. Executes normally for an entire group of considered Locations (e.g. entire Facility) on a fixed date at the end of a defined period (e.g. accounting year). This method is supported by tracking the "Last Stocktaking Date" of the configured stocktaking object which thus can be identified as already finished (counted) within a mentioned period.

Perpetual Stocktaking

(Perpetual Inventory, Permanent Inventory, Continuous Inventory)

Method to determine stock levels (quantities) in the context of a basic Stocktaking process. Executes incrementally during the complete accounting year, respectively considered period. Provided this is appropriately implemented, the remaining number of unchecked Stocktaking Objects at the end of the considered period should be marginal. Usually, Perpetual stocktaking can be performed in different execution types that can exist together:

  • Perpetual Stocktaking ad hoc explicit during first storing
    (stock level determination executes during the first storing of Inventory into a considered location).
  • Perpetual Stocktaking ad hoc explicit during picking
    (    stock level determination executes during a pick operation, respectively during retrieval operation, provided that the current stock level at the considered Location is zero or nearly zero after the pick).
  • Perpetual Stocktaking implicit during receiving
    (counting of Inventory can be caused by other reasons, e.g. while receiving or clearing of faults. If Inventory is explicitly counted, the stocktaking date of the considered Load or Inventory Unit updates due to implicit Stocktaking).
  • Perpetual Stocktaking with an explicit planned Counting Order
    (stock level determination executes incrementally for the considered group of Locations during the complete accounting year by generating periodic counting instructions for a subset of considered Locations or SKUs. Each Stocktaking object is counted, at most, one time inside the considered period).
