Overview Pick Controller (PIC)

Overview

The Pick Controller subsystem (PIC) is the central task execution subsystem concerning all picking processes. Communication with the Logistics Manager is generally through the following two channels:

  • Order Fulfillment provides Pick Requests to the Pick Controller
  • Pick Controller provides Pick Confirmations and other process information to Order Fulfillment.

The architecture allows multiple Pick Controllers to be used in parallel. Depending on the Picking Zone, the Order Fulfillment subsystem addresses the adequate Pick Controller and submits the picking workload to it as Pick Orders.


Basic responsibilities of a Pick Controller:

  • Pick Planning and Optimization (e.g. Order selection for Multi-Order Picking, optimization of Pick Routes)
  • Pick Execution (e.g. execution of all Picks of a Pick Walk)
  • Equipment configuration (e.g. setup and configuration of a Pick-To-Light system, configuration of Pick Carts for Multi-Order Route based Picking)
  • Communication with Picking equipment (e.g. Pick list printing for Paper-based Picking, control of displays in Pick-to-Light systems, requesting and confirmation of required Load Unit transports at a Goods-to-Person Pickstation for pallets and containers)
  • Perpetual stocktaking for Pick Faces or source pallets (e.g. confirmation of empty location counting and confirmation of residual inventory in a Pick Face after picking)
  • Handling of Pick Containers (e.g. selection, identification and association of a new container for an Order during a Pick Route, releasing a full and adding a new container in a Pick-to-Light system)
  • Delivery of Pick Containers to predefined destinations (e.g. delivery to a follow-up Pick Area or a packing station)
  • Capturing of performance data.

 

Two main Pick Controller Types:

  • Person-to-Goods Picking (Route based Picking. Deals with all Picking processes special to manually operated Areas where Pickers are guided to Storage Locations for Pick operations from the Pick Face into the manually transported Pick Containers).
  • Goods-to-Person Picking (Deals with all Picking processes special to automated Areas where goods are transported to stationary Pickstations for Pick operations and subsequently conveyed back to the Storage Area).

Each Pick Controller uses basic functionality provided by a general subsystem:

  • general operative business objects (entities) and services for Picking and general configurations
  • general parts of planning and execution of Picking processes.
