Introduction

Overview Material Flow Controller

Purpose

The Material Flow Controller (MFC) is responsible for route planning control and supervision of transport processes within a warehouse.
For this purpose, it offers a standardized interface to superimposed systems that can be used to submit transport requests, to receive transport confirmations and to transmit status information that the MFC has received from transport devices through their respective controls layer.
Normally, the MFC executes transport requests on request of Logistics Manager.

Functionality

From the perspective of the end user, the major functions of the Material Flow Control system are:

  • Receive transport requests from a superimposed system and confirm these after execution
  • Control of material flow
  • Generation and administration of transport orders according to priority, sequence, status, and other parameters
  • Management of equipment status and consideration of status information for route planning
  • Buffer control: Transports are released only if the destination can be reached. Actual limitations, such as limited buffer capacities (e.g. at a workstation), equipment failure, capacity and utilization of required equipment are considered for transport order release
  • Reaction on equipment failure during transportation
  • Communication with subordinate systems using the Standard Dematic Controls Interface protocol (DCI)
  • Information, statistics, and management reports related to material flow.
