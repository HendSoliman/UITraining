Material Flow Management

Overview

Material Flow Management deals with the administration and the lifecycle of Transport Requests and their delegation to underlying transport subsystems for execution as well as the management of de-palletizing processes. After the execution of a Transport Request by a transport execution system, the Transport Request Management confirms the transport to the superior business process of the Logistics Manager. For a unified workflow control, each Transport Request has an associated Task of appropriate Task Type which is administrated by the Workflow Management System.

Transports

Transport Request creation occurs within superior business cases, e.g. while finishing the receiving process of a full pallet or at the start of the retrieval process of all Load Units for a Shipping Order. A Transport Request is always related to a Load Unit which must be transported from a Source to a Destination Location. The Transport Request is delegated to an underlying subsystem for execution, e.g. to the MFC which then complete the data of the overall Transport with data of the Transport Order.

A Transport Request must have a Source and a current Location. The Current Location is continuously updated when the underlying transport subsystem reports a new current location. A Transport Request usually has an initial destination also. This may be a precise or rough destination, e.g. the Virtual Location of an Area. During a transport, the destination becomes more detailed until a precise Location is defined.

Before a Transport Request is sent to the underlying transport subsystem, the Putaway strategy determines a destination if it was not set during creation and if the Area is in "auto dispo mode". The granularity of the initially requested destination (Area, Aisle, bin) is configured in the Source Area. If no destination can be found, e.g. storage is full, the Transport Request is released and sent to the underlying transport subsystem using the Default Destination of the current Location.

To avoid damages, palettes may be relocated inside an automatic high bay storage aisle a limited number of times. If the number of Aisle internal relocations was exceeded, MFM moves the load unit first out of the aisle. Then, the underlying transport subsystem can request a new destination for the subsequent inbound move.

Transport Requests to the same destination can be grouped together and a group may be sequenced.

Depalletizing

Depalletizing is the repacking of Inventory from a pallet into a container or onto a belt. This manually planned segregation of aggregated units occurs at specific Segregation Workstations - Depalletizing Workstations. These Workstations receive aggregated units, which may be fully or partly segregated, and output a set of segregated units and optionally a remainder of the input.

Automatic depalletizing (e.g. specifically used in AMCAP scenarios) is done with Depalletizer Stations.

Depalletizing Workstations can be used for the replenishment of Miniload or Multishuttle Areas from pallets or for a Sorter induction. Supported types are the De-palletizing into trays or totes or directly to belt or conveyor. Depalletized units may be identified by a unique Load Unit ID or by a non-unique ID, such as SKU ID, ZIP Code, other.

Depalletizing deals with:

  • configuration of Depalletizing Workstations
  • pallet assignment pallets to Depalletizing Workstations
  • screen for Depalletizing operations
  • booking Depalletizing confirmations
  • supply of all these mechanisms to any business process which requires a segregation step.

QA Decisions

The support of material flow decisions for Load Units, which are based on criteria related to quality assurance (QA), is provided through QA Decision Points. At these decision points within the material flow system, the next destination of a Load Unit is determined in function of QA criteria. For a QA condition, a Load Unit is directed towards one of the QA destinations depending on specific QA criteria; otherwise towards the destination corresponding to the normal flow.
